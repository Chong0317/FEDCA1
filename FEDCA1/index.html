The Five Zero-Waste Principles

  1. Refuse: Say no to single-use plastics and freebies that add clutter.
    • Decline plastic straws when dining out.
    • Opt out of free samples or promotional leaflets.
    • Avoid over-packaged products.
  2. Reduce: Buy less, choose quality over quantity, and simplify your life.
    • Purchase bulk items to cut down on packaging waste.
    • Resist fast-fashion impulse buys.
    • Choose digital receipts over paper ones.
  3. Reuse: Repair and repurpose items instead of tossing them.
    • Drink from a refillable water bottle.
    • Bring reusable grocery bags.
    • Store pantry staples in glass jars.
  4. Recycle: Properly sort materials to keep them out of landfills.
    • Recycle plastics, paper, and metals.
    • Take e-waste to certified collection points.
    • Donate or recycle old magazines and newspapers.
  5. Compost: Turn organic scraps into soil-enriching compost.
    • Compost fruit and vegetable peelings.
    • Add used coffee grounds to your pile.
    • Include eggshells and yard clippings.
